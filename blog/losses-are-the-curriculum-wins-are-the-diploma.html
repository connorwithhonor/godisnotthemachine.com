The Losses Are the Curriculum. The Wins Are Just the Diploma.

Everything we talked about today. AI models that cost $14 billion. Food engineered in labs to keep you addicted. An industry that charges you more to sell your home because they can. Every single one of those systems was designed by people chasing a lowercase g. Money. Power. Market share. Click-through rate.

An uppercase G does not promise you it will avoid the storm. It promises you that you will not face it alone. That is the difference. That is the only difference that matters. Every system chasing a lowercase g is designed to extract from you. To take your attention, your money, your health, your peace. An uppercase G asks nothing from you except that you show up. Broken is fine. Angry is fine. Confused is fine. Just show up.

Twenty years as a cop. The worst people can do to each other. Doors at three in the morning that nobody should ever have to knock on. Families destroyed. Lives ended. The things that crawl into your head at night and refuse to leave. And through all of it, the one thing that kept me from becoming the cynical, burnt-out, angry version of myself that this world tries to turn you into. One simple thing.

Gratitude.

Not for the good stuff. That is easy. Anybody can be grateful when the deal closes, when the scale moves in the right direction, when the business has a good week. That costs you nothing.

The real work is gratitude for the bad stuff. For the hard calls. For the days you wanted to quit. For the fat you were carrying and the fat you continue to carry right now. For the businesses that failed before the ones that worked. For the marriage that ended. For the career that did not go where you planned. For the person who betrayed your trust. For the diagnosis you did not expect.

Because those were the days that built you. Not the wins. The wins are the diploma. They look nice on the wall. But the losses are the curriculum. The losses are where the actual learning happened. The losses are where you found out what you are made of.

If all you are grateful for is the wins, you have not learned anything yet.

Whether your anchor is Jesus, like mine, or something else you hold on to when everything gets loud. Find it today and hold on to it. Because the noise out there is only getting louder. And the people selling the noise do not care if you make it through.

The CEOs chasing the lowercase g do not care about the 22-year-old whose career got automated. The food companies chasing the lowercase g do not care about the 317-pound man hiding fast food under his front seat. The real estate industry chasing the lowercase g does not care that you overpaid by $20,000 in commissions on your home sale.

They are all chasing a lowercase g. And lowercase gods always let you down. Because they were never designed to hold you up. They were designed to hold themselves up. At your expense.

An uppercase G does not need your money, your clicks, your subscription, or your failure to stay in business. An uppercase G just needs you to show up. And when you do, even when the storm is raging, you discover something remarkable. You are not alone in it. You never were.

AI for everyone. Not just the wealthy. Recovery for everyone. Not just the ones who can afford a clinic. Fair pricing for everyone. Not just the ones who know how to negotiate. That is the mission. And that is why we show up.
